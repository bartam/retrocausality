Implications

  • Free will is not what we think it is.

    Your choices may be constrained by a future that has already decided its shape.

  • Memory is not a record — it's a negotiation.

    If the future alters the past, then what you remember may be a compromise between what happened and what will happen.

  • Prophecy becomes physics.

    Premonitions, déjà vu, and oracular traditions could be imperfect perceptions of retrocausal information leaking backward.

  • Death is not an ending.

    In a block universe, every moment you have ever occupied still exists. You are always there.
