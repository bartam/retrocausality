They Knew This in History

Some texts survived. Each describes its own method.

The Lesser Key of Solomon
Ars Goetia, 17th century

Method: Seal yourself inside a drawn circle. Vocalize names in fixed rhythm without pause. Hold the desired outcome in total concentration. The entity is bound to deliver it.

The text states the result may manifest before the conjuration is complete.
Liber Null
Peter J. Carroll, 1978

Method: Enter gnosis through sensory extremity — pain, exhaustion, ecstasy. Encode intent into a sigil. At peak state, focus on the sigil completely. Then destroy it and forget.

Carroll describes this explicitly as "reaching backward along the causal chain."
The Picatrix
Ghāyat al-Ḥakīm, c. 1000 AD

Method: Align operations to celestial windows where time is described as "thin." Prepare specific materials. Enter a prescribed mental state. Release the working at the calculated moment.

The Vatican holds two copies. Both are restricted access. No reason given.
