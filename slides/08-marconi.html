The Marconi Anomaly

Cornwall, England — 1901

During Marconi's first transatlantic radio test, his assistant George Kemp logged a second signal — received fourteen minutes before the transmission was sent from Poldhu.


Kemp noted the anomaly in his personal journal but Marconi ordered it struck from the official log, calling it "atmospheric echo."


Kemp's journal surfaced at a Sotheby's auction in 1987. The entry has never been explained.

The Montauk Letters

Long Island, New York — 1943–1983

Among the Philadelphia Experiment folklore, one detail is consistently overlooked: a series of letters between two Navy physicists, found in the National Archives in 2003 and quietly re-sealed.


The letters describe a "temporal displacement field" and reference measurements that preceded their own calibration by hours.


The archivists' log shows 14 FOIA requests for this file since 2003. All denied.
